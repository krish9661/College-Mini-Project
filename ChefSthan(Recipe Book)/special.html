Kanda Poha

Poha Recipe Ingredients :-

  • Poha: I prefer Medium or Thick poha to make this recipe. When rinsed, it gets fluffy and absorbent. It is easily available in Indian grocery stores. The thin poha doesn't work for this recipe, as it turns mushy when rinsed. You may also use red or brown rice poha.
  • Onion: I used finely chopped red onion. You can also use yellow or white onions.
  • Potatoes: I used one medium Yukon gold potato. However, you can make this poha without potatoes as well.
  • Spices: a tempering (tadka) made using mustard seeds, cumin seeds (jeera), curry leaves, and green chilies. I use turmeric powder for color and flavor.
  • Peanuts: You can add peanuts for the crunch. Oil: You can use any cooking oil.

    • Procedure :-
  • Gently rinse poha in a colander under running water 2-3 times until they soften, and then drain out all the water. When you press with your fingers, it should break easily. Keep it aside.
  • Heat oil in a Kadai. Add raw peanuts and fry until they turn golden brown. Remove the peanuts to a plate and set them aside.
  • In the same kadai, add mustard and cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Then, add green chilies, curry leaves, and chopped onion. Saute until onions turn soft and translucent.
  • Next, add cubed potatoes (or mixed vegetables) and cook for 2-3 minutes. To make simple plain pohe, you can skip adding potatoes or veggies.
  • Cover and cook on medium-low heat till the potatoes are soft and perfectly cooked. Sprinkle some water if required. (You can also use pre-boiled cubed potatoes and saute for 2 minutes).
  • Add rinsed and drained poha, turmeric, salt, and sugar. Mix everything well.
  • If you feel the pohe is too dry, sprinkle some water. Cover it with a lid and cook for 2 minutes on low heat.
  • Turn off the heat. Add the lemon juice and roasted peanuts, and give a quick mix. Lastly, garnish with coriander leaves.


    • Serve hot with Masala chai or Coffee

    Butter Paneer Masala

    Butter Paneer Masala Ingredients :-

  • Ripe, red & juicy tomatoes: Tomatoes are a key ingredient here and form the base of the makhani sauce. As such, it is important to choose good, ripe tomatoes that are sweet.
  • Raw Cashews: Yet another important ingredient for the gravy is cashews. The nuts impart a lovely creaminess and sheen in the dish, and the sweetness of the cashews help to balance the tanginess of the tomatoes.
  • Cream: In addition to using cashew paste, this Butter Paneer recipe calls for cream to help thicken the sauce and add richness.
  • Butter: The amount of butter that is added is just right in this paneer butter masala recipe. Butter makes the curry luxurious and, well… buttery.
  • Paneer: The quality of your paneer can make or break your dish. What you want are succulent, soft paneer cubes gently coated with a smooth, buttery tomato sauce.
  • Spices & Herbs: One reason this recipe is so great for weeknights is that the list of herbs and spices isn’t intimidatingly long. For that brilliant orange color, you need to add Kashmiri red chilli powder.

    • Procedure :-
  • Heat 1 teaspoon of oil in a pan on medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves and saute for few seconds.Then add the the onion, garlic, ginger and saute for 2 to 3 minutes until the onion is translucent.
  • Add the tomatoes and cashews and mix.
  • Then add 1 cup of water.
  • Cover the pan and cook on medium heat for 15 minutes.
  • After 15 minutes, remove the pan from heat. Remove the bay leaf, cinnamon stick and cloves.
  • Let the mixture cool down a bit and then transfer to a blender. It’s important to let it cool down a bit else it will all blow up from the mixer.
  • Grind the masala to a smooth paste and set aside.
  • Grind the masala to a smooth paste and set aside.
  • Once the butter melts, add the red chili powder and the Kashmiri red chili powder and fry for few seconds. This will give the curry a nice red color.
  • Then add the ground paste back into the pan along with the garam masala (start with 1/2 teaspoon and add the remaining 1/4 teaspoon at the end only if you feel like the curry needs that extra bit of garam masala), cardamom powder, sugar, salt and tomato paste (if using).
  • Then add the cream and mix
  • Add in the paneer and cook for 2 to 3 minutes on medium heat. Finally add crushed kasuri methi.


    • Garnish paneer butter masala with coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice!

    Samosa

    Samosa Ingredients :-
    For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida, 260 grams)
  • 1 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 4 tablespoons oil (60 ml + 5 ml, plus 1 teaspoon)
  • 6 tablespoons water (for kneading the dough, approximately)
    • For the Filling:
  • 4 medium potatoes (500-550 grams)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • ¼ teaspoon hing (asafoetida)
  • ½ cup green peas (soaked in warm water if using frozen peas)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon amchur (dried mango powder)
  • ¼ teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ¾ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
    • Instructions:
  • Prepare the Dough: Mix all-purpose flour, ajwain, salt, and 4 tablespoons of oil. Rub the oil into the flour for about 3-4 minutes until well incorporated. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  • Prepare the Filling: Boil, peel, and mash the potatoes. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, fennel seeds, crushed coriander seeds, ginger, green chili, and hing. Add green peas and cook for a few minutes. Mix in the mashed potatoes, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until well combined. Let the filling cool.
  • Assemble the Samosas: Divide the dough into small balls. Roll out each ball into a thin circle. Cut the circle in half to form two semi-circles. Fold each semi-circle into a cone shape, sealing the edges with water. Fill the cone with the potato mixture. Seal the top by pressing the edges together.
  • Fry the Samosas: Heat oil for deep frying. Fry the samosas on medium heat until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  • Serve Hot: Serve your homemade samosas with mint chutney or tamarind chutney. Enjoy the flaky goodness!

    Mushroom Masala

    Mushroom Masala Ingredients :-

  • 230 grams (8 ounces) sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup cubed onions (about 1 large onion)
  • 1 cup cubed tomatoes (about 2 large tomatoes)
  • ½ inch ginger, chopped
  • cloves garlic, chopped
  • 12 cashew nuts (or almonds)
  • 2 tablespoons oil (or butter)
  • teaspoon garam masala
  • ¾ teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder (optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon salt (or as needed)
  • ¼ teaspoon kasuri methi (optional, dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 small bay leaf (or 10 curry leaves)
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1-inch cinnamon piece
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • 3 tablespoons cream (optional)
    • Instructions:
  • Boil the onions in water for 4-5 minutes until transparent. Drain and blend them with tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and cashews (or almonds). This step removes the raw smell and bitter taste from the onions.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add bay leaf, green cardamoms, and cinnamon.
  • Pour in the onion-tomato puree.
  • Add garam masala, red chili powder, coriander powder (optional), salt, and kasuri methi.
  • Add sliced mushrooms and cook until tender.
  • Finish with chopped coriander leaves and cream (optional).
  • Serve with rice, roti, or naan.


    • Enjoy this delicious Mushroom Masala Curry! 😊
